1. Parenting ^mow9N2sr

Society Protocol operates as an "invite-only" protocol (meaning: individuals actors need to either be a part of the genesis actors or be invited into the instance by someone who already has an account to create a new actor.

Parents can allocate energy to inviting children. After the invite, a new account is created for the child and is tethered to the parent account. The tether forms a family tree and creates a tax on the child that when it earns energy some of it goes up the family tree to the parents. ^JFGdRcjT

Mechanism ^tOM9Sgg3

Meaning ^0rJ6oZvq

The parenting level is a mechanism designed to incentivize the invitation of only individuals perceived as valuable into the society. 

It is a risk level mimicking parenting in real life and forms family trees.  

The parent risks (pays initially to potentially recoup more over time) some amount of energy to invite someone they perceive as being valuable to society (hoping it's a good child) which initially costs energy, but can recoup a lot more over time (hoping that the child takes care of them as they grow old) if it is a good child.  

Risking energy to invite children serves the dual function of balancing the individual desire with societal interests:

For the individual actor (parent): Allows the earning of energy by finding and reffering valuable participants to the system.

For the societal pyramid: We are incentivizing only the addition of actors perceived as valuable. Thereby, only filling our society with the best potential participants. ^blUobfmt

Energy Flows ^Lo3nqQJT

new child account (+). 

Afterwards, the child is tethered to the parent, whenever the child earns
energy, some percentage of it flows from the child (-) up the family tree (+).
